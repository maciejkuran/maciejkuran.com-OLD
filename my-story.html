My story.

Front-end web developer

Hi, I am Maciej Kuran-Janowski, a self-taught programmer and designer from Poland.

I was born on 1993-10-27 in Warsaw, Poland. I graduated from music school, hospitality technical college, and then Nicolai Copernicus University with a bachelor's degree in homeland security. I am happily married, owning 2 amazing pugs. You may ask, why programming and such a life change? After six years of being an entrepreneur, I craved change.

In 2021 I needed to re-organize my life, to figure out what's next and what I want my professional life to look like for the next decades. I felt like there was a big gap in my current professional life and I needed an activity that would fulfill me and unleash my creativity.

Innumerable ideas

I thought of innumerable ideas but none of them made sense to me except programming and web design/development.

I started from scratch. Reading tons of articles and going through tutorials, helped me begin my WordPress adventure in 2021. I learned so much in the first year of developing business websites & e-commerce stores for many clients.

Starting from HTML and CSS

I know, I didn't surprise you but I guess that's the way how everyone else started. I found a highly rated course that was led by Daniel Walter Scott who owns "Bring Your Own Laptop" course platform. He is an Adobe Certified Instructor. I picked him because the course structure looked just simply great for a beginner. Not too much, not too little. Also, his attitude and teaching skills are awesome. This was a great choice - no regrets.

If someone thinks that one course should set you in the position of becoming a "pro". It doesn't work like that. I expected that this course will help me to understand coding basics in HTML and CSS, but a real challenge starts afterward. By going up through this course I realized, how much is to learn but it didn't make me feel overwhelmed even for a second. I wanted to push coding further.

My first serious coding commission

It was for my dad. I wasn't asked to do it, but I offered my dad to design and develop his basic business website. The site was supposed to be clean and mobile-friendly. I was excited.

The goal of this project was basically to accomplish it. I know that it sounds weird but at that time I didn't feel confident in basic stuff, such as flexbox, media queries and so on.

Today I realize how many code repetitions I have done, and how many headaches I have caused myself by using strange practices. Well, I didn't expect to follow the Don't repeat yourself principle as the goal was to develop it to the point where everything works.

PS. Actually, at that time I had no idea about the DRY principle existence.

I completed the project and deployed the files on a server. You may see this website here.

Learning SASS - SCSS

One day I was reading about how powerful SASS is. I had mixed feelings because I didn't feel like learning something new unless I am 100 % confident in CSS. However, I gave it a try as I realized that SCSS is an "extended version of CSS". I can't complain now as working with scss sheets organizes my style sheets and increases workflow.

Learning Bootstrap

Well, honestly I wasn't excited going up through the process of learning the basics of this framework. Copying and pasting the code, and styling with pre-made classes sort of brought me a DEJA VU feeling from WordPress times where I used to develop websites with builders and plugins. This wasn't fun! ( I mean it was but just at the beginning).

However, I completely understand the concept of using Bootstrap. If you want to have the visual aspects of the website done fast, or maybe you work under time pressure, or maybe the project agreements made with the Client including using Bootstrap as the HTML, CSS, and JS library - then this is a great choice.

However, so far my design concepts were far from default Bootstrap ones, so I might have to almost redesign everything. That's pointless. Also copying and pasting is not my current programming target so I said "see you later" to Bootstrap :)

Anyway, I know what Bootstrap is about and can use it anytime where it's necessary.

Vanilla Javascript - the beginnings

Starting to learn javascript was a tough time. After all, javascript is a real programming language.

The first course that I decided to pick was "The Creative Javascript Course" for beginners by DevelopedByEd.
Dev Ed is a recognizable, funny YouTuber. His attitude and personality encouraged me to go with him as my first course.

However, it didn't work out as I decided to pause this course somewhere in the middle.

I will emphasize that this is not Ed's fault as he can undoubtedly teach and is a great js programmer. In my opinion, the course was not for me because it was geared towards building web applications alone. There was a lack of complex discussion of js concepts, and connecting concepts.

What I felt I was doing wrong from the beginning was taking tons of notes and learning multiple lines of code by heart. It is not how it should be!

A programmer's job is creativity. Creating solutions to existing problems. I realized that you don't have to copy someone else's code to achieve the same goal. You can write it your way. You need to know the concepts and let them match a problem.

Becoming confident - Javascript bootcamp with Jonas Schmedtmann

Jonas Schmedtmann Bootcamp is an amazing opportunity for each future developer. The course structure is just great. The amount of time and effort that Jonas put into course development and several concepts explanations - is just amazing.

What I specifically love about this course are assignments and coding challenges with a ton of different programming problems to solve! I just loved it. No matter how much time I needed to spend on solving one particular, I still remember this feeling once I solved a specific problem successfully on my own.

Day by day my confidence started to increase.

Programming is about connecting concepts

Programming is about connecting concepts. I understood by solving problems by myself, sometimes with a bit of research which is fine. Getting stuck for hours was a part of the plan.

I realized that every program, no matter how big or small, is built up of connected concepts. It sounds a bit trivial but well, it took me some time to realize it. Nothing comes easy.
